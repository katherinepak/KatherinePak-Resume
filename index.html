Katherine Pak

Linguistics

To obtain a degree in Linguistics and engage in research opportunities and job internships to best prepare for a career as a speech-language pathologist. I have always been confident and ardent in leading and teaching others, so I hope to further my knowledge and learn more about speech pathology in different settings.

Some of my leadership positions include an intern of choreography for a Korean Christian competitive dance ministry called Seeds of Worship. I have been actively performing since 2010, teaching since 2006, and have taken on a job as a dance instructor this past fall. I also closely mentored and taught dance choreography to students with learning disabilities. Through these past commitments, I have discovered that I have the passion, patience, and charisma necessary to work with individuals of all backgrounds. I have been exposed to working with children and adults of all backgrounds, and I hope to make an impact in my community by aiding those who struggle with speech and language communication problems. I have past involvements as board members in Girl's League and Jaggo, which were school clubs on my high school campus. As the Publicity Officer in the club Jaggo, I created visually appealing graphics to promote the organization on social media and our school website. I also volunteered at crisis shelters and helped run meetings where we educated over forty club members on feminist issues. Through Girl's League, I was given the opportunity to educate club members on empowering women of all backgrounds and promoting feminism through volunteer work and social events.

I hope that through the knowledge I have retained from my past involvements, I can continue to learn and aid others in their struggle to communicate with others. My goal is to substantially make an impact in my community whether it's through my studies or interactions with patients, and I hope that I can put my learning into practice and begin my journey soon.
